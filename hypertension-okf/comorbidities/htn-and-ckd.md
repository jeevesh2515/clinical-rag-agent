# Hypertension in Chronic Kidney Disease

## BP Targets

| CKD Stage | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| CKD with albuminuria (ACR >30 mg/g) | <130/80 |
| CKD without albuminuria | <140/90 |
| ALLHAT / SPRINT subgroups | Intensive targets safe if tolerated |

## Drug Selection

### First-Line (always)
- **ACEi** or **ARB** — renoprotective effect independent of BP lowering
- Titrate to **maximum tolerated dose** (not just target BP dose)
- Preferred regardless of race

### Add-Ons (if above goal on ACEi/ARB max dose)
| Step | Agent | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Add | CCB (amlodipine) | Well-tolerated in CKD |
| Add | Thiazide-like (chlorthalidone) | Effective if eGFR ≥30 |
| If eGFR <30 | Loop diuretic (furosemide, torsemide) | Replace thiazide |

### Drugs to Avoid / Use with Caution

| Drug | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **K-sparing diuretics** (spironolactone, eplerenone) | Risk of hyperkalemia, especially with ACEi/ARB + CKD |
| **NSAIDs** | Blunt ACEi/ARB effect; risk of AKI |
| **Atenolol** (renally cleared) | Accumulates in CKD; prefer bisoprolol if BB needed |

## Monitoring

| Parameter | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| Serum creatinine + eGFR | 1–2 weeks after ACEi/ARB start or dose change; then q3–6 months |
| Serum potassium | Same as creatinine (especially with ACEi/ARB + CKD) |
| ACR (albumin:creatinine ratio) | At least annually |
| BP | Every visit; home BP monitoring encouraged |

### Acceptable Increase in Creatinine
- Up to **≤30% increase** in creatinine after starting ACEi/ARB is acceptable and renoprotective
- >30% increase: hold, recheck, evaluate for volume depletion or [[pharmacology/contraindications|bilateral RAS]]
