# Hypertension in Diabetes

## BP Targets

| Guideline | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| ACC/AHA 2017 | <130/80 |
| ESC/ESH 2023 | <130/80 |
| NICE NG136 | <140/90 (clinic) |
| ADA 2024 | <130/80 (if high CVD risk, <130/80 preferred) |

## Drug Selection

### First-Line
- **ACEi** or **ARB** — preferred in diabetes with albuminuria
- If ACR <30 and no CKD, can start with ACEi/ARB, CCB, or thiazide-like

### Add-Ons
| Step | Agent | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Add | CCB or thiazide-like | If not at goal on ACEi/ARB max dose |
| Add | Third agent from different class | As per [[protocols/step-care-treatment]] |

### Agents with Caution

| Drug | Issue |
|------|-------|
| **Beta-blockers** | May mask hypoglycemia symptoms (tachycardia); use cardioselective (bisoprolol, metoprolol) if needed |
| **Thiazide diuretics** | Can worsen glucose control (mild, dose-dependent); benefit still outweighs risk |
| **High-dose loop diuretics** | May cause volume depletion → AKI |

## Additional Risk Management

- **Statin** — recommended for all diabetic patients with HTN
- **Low-dose aspirin** — consider if ASCVD risk ≥10% (not for primary prevention in all)
- **SGLT2 inhibitor or GLP-1 agonist** — if HF or CKD, independent of glycemic control
