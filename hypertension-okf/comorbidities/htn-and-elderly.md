# Hypertension in the Elderly

## BP Targets by Age

| Age group | Guideline | Target |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| 65–79, healthy | ACC/AHA | <130/80 (if tolerated) |
| 65–79, healthy | ESC/ESH | <140/80 (aim <130/80 if tolerated) |
| 65–79, healthy | NICE | <140/90 (clinic) |
| ≥80 | ACC/AHA | <130/80 (if ambulatory and tolerated) |
| ≥80 | ESC/ESH | <150/90 (aim <140/80 if tolerated) |
| ≥80 | NICE | <150/90 (clinic) |
| Frail / limited life expectancy | All | Individualize; avoid aggressive targets |

## Special Considerations

### Start Low, Go Slow
- Start any antihypertensive at **half the usual starting dose**
- Titrate every **4–6 weeks** (not 2–4)
- Monitor for orthostatic hypotension at every visit (check standing BP at 1 and 3 minutes)

### Preferred Drug Classes

| Condition | Preferred | Avoid / Caution |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Isolated systolic HTN | CCB or thiazide-like diuretic | — |
| Falls risk / frailty | CCB (amlodipine) | Alpha-blockers (doxazosin) — ↑ falls risk |
| Osteoporosis | Thiazide-like (↑ bone density) | — |
| BPH | Alpha-blocker (tamsulosin, doxazosin) | Beta-blockers may worsen |
| Orthostatic hypotension | Avoid aggressive dosing | Alpha-blockers, diuretics (if volume depleted) |

### Drugs to Use with Caution
- **Alpha-blockers** (doxazosin, terazosin) — orthostatic hypotension, falls
- **Verapamil** — constipation (common in elderly)
- **Spironolactone** — hyperkalemia risk (especially if CKD)
- **Beta-blockers** — bradycardia, fatigue, depression

## Monitoring

| Check | Frequency | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Standing BP | Every visit | At 1 and 3 minutes |
| Creatinine/eGFR | 1–2 weeks after starting/titrating ACEi/ARB/diuretic | Then q6 months |
| K+, Na+ | 1–2 weeks after starting/titrating | Then q6 months |
| Orthostatic symptoms | Every visit | Dizziness, lightheadedness, falls |
| Cognitive function | Baseline and if concern | Aggressive BP lowering may rarely cause cognitive decline |
