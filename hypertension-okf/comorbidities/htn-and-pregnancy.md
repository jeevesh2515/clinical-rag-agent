# Hypertension in Pregnancy

## Classification of HTN in Pregnancy

| Type | Onset | Features |
|------|-------|----------|
| Chronic HTN | <20 weeks or pre-existing | BP ≥140/90 before pregnancy or <20 weeks |
| Gestational HTN | ≥20 weeks | New-onset, no proteinuria |
| Preeclampsia | ≥20 weeks | HTN + proteinuria (or end-organ dysfunction) |
| Chronic HTN + superimposed preeclampsia | ≥20 weeks | Worsening BP + new proteinuria/features |

## BP Targets in Pregnancy

| Condition | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Chronic HTN (no comorbidity) | Treat if ≥160/105; avoid aggressive lowering |
| Chronic HTN + target organ damage | Consider treating at ≥140/90 |
| Gestational HTN / Pre-eclampsia | Treat if ≥140/90 (NICE); target <135/85 |
| Severe HTN in pregnancy | Treat immediately if ≥160/110 |

## Safe Antihypertensives in Pregnancy

| Drug | Dose | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **Labetalol** | 100–600 mg PO BID (or IV for severe) | First-line; safe in all trimesters |
| **Nifedipine LA** | 30–90 mg PO daily | First-line alternative; avoid sublingual |
| **Methyldopa** | 250–750 mg PO BID | First-line but less preferred due to side effects (drowsiness) |

## Drugs Contraindicated in Pregnancy

| Drug | Risk |
|------|------|
| **ACE inhibitors** (captopril, lisinopril, ramipril, etc.) | Fetal renal dysplasia, oligohydramnios, skull ossification defects (2nd/3rd trimester) |
| **ARBs** (losartan, valsartan, etc.) | Same as ACEi |
| **Spironolactone** | Anti-androgenic effects in fetus |
| **Atenolol** | Fetal growth restriction with chronic use |

## When to Refer to Obstetric Medicine

- BP not controlled despite ≥2 agents
- Suspected secondary HTN in pregnancy
- Preeclampsia with severe features
- HTN + CKD or diabetes
- Any HTN presenting <20 weeks
