# Hypertensive Urgency vs Emergency

## Decision Tree

```
BP ≥180/120 (severe elevation)
│
├── With acute target organ damage?
│   │
│   YES ────────────────────────────── NO
│   │                                   │
│   ▼                                   ▼
│   HYPERTENSIVE EMERGENCY              HYPERTENSIVE URGENCY
│   (End-organ damage present)          (No acute end-organ damage)
│   │                                   │
│   ├── Encephalopathy / stroke         ├── Severe headache
│   ├── Acute coronary syndrome         ├── Anxiety
│   ├── Acute pulmonary edema            ├── No symptoms
│   ├── Aortic dissection               │
│   ├── Acute renal failure             ├── Management:
│   ├── Retinopathy (hemorrhages)       │   Restart or intensify oral
│   ├── Eclampsia                       │   medications. Do NOT need
│   │                                   │   rapid IV reduction.
│   ├── Management:                     │   Can be managed outpatient.
│   │   IV therapy                     │   Recheck BP in 24–48 hours.
│   │   ICU admission required          │
│   │   Lower BP by max 25% in 1 hr     │
│   │   Avoid rapid decrease            │
```

## Drug Therapy — Hypertensive Emergency

| Condition | Preferred Agent | Dose | Target |
|-----------|----------------|------|--------|
| Encephalopathy/stroke | Labetalol IV | 10–20 mg IVP q10min or 0.5–2 mg/min infusion | ↓25% in 1 hour; avoid rapid drop |
| Aortic dissection | **Esmolol** (first) + **Nitroprusside** or **Nicardipine** | Esmolol 50–200 mcg/kg/min; target HR <60 | SBP 100–120 within 20 min |
| Acute coronary syndrome | Nitroglycerin IV + Labetalol IV | NTG 5–200 mcg/min; Labetalol 10–20 mg | ↓25% in 1 hour |
| Acute pulmonary edema | Nitroglycerin IV + Nitroprusside IV | NTG 5–200 mcg/min; SNP 0.25–10 mcg/kg/min | ↓25% in 1 hour |
| Eclampsia/severe preeclampsia | Labetalol IV or Hydralazine IV or Nifedipine immediate-release PO | Labetalol 10–20 mg IV; Hydralazine 5–10 mg IV; Nifedipine 10 mg PO | SBP <160, DBP <105 |

## Drug Therapy — Hypertensive Urgency

| Drug | Dose | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Restart or up-titrate home medications | — | First-line approach |
| Labetalol | 100–400 mg PO (if no bradycardia/heart block) | Single dose, reassess |
| Captopril | 12.5–25 mg PO | Short-acting ACEi |
| Clonidine | 0.1–0.2 mg PO | Avoid in elderly, falls risk |
| Amlodipine | 5–10 mg PO | Slower onset, sustained effect |

**Do NOT use nifedipine immediate-release capsules** — risk of uncontrolled hypotension, reflex tachycardia, stroke, MI.
