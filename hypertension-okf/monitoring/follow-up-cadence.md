# Follow-Up Cadence

## Follow-Up Interval After Treatment Change

| Scenario | Interval |
|----------|----------|
| After starting medication | 2–4 weeks |
| After dose titration | 2–4 weeks |
| After adding second agent | 2–4 weeks |
| After adding third agent | 2–4 weeks |
| BP controlled, stable regimen | 3–6 months |
| BP controlled, elderly or frail | 3–6 months (or sooner per clinician) |

## Follow-Up Visit Components

### Every Visit
- BP measurement (seated, after 5 min rest; standing if elderly/diabetic)
- Medication adherence check (ask, don't assume)
- Side effect screening
- Home BP log review (if available)
- Lifestyle counseling review

### Every 6–12 Months
- Serum creatinine + eGFR
- Serum potassium, sodium
- Fasting glucose or HbA1c (if diabetes or prediabetes)
- Lipid panel (annually)
- Urinalysis (for proteinuria)

### Annually
- Electrocardiogram (if LVH suspected, new symptoms, or q1–2 years)
- ASCVD risk score (if not already high-risk)
- Medication reconciliation
- Referral update (if previously deferred)

## When to Escalate Follow-Up

| Finding | Action |
|---------|--------|
| BP ≥20/10 above goal | Consider escalating or adding agent; recheck in 1–2 weeks |
| Newly elevated creatinine (>30% from baseline) | Hold ACEi/ARB, recheck in 1 week |
| K+ >5.5 | Hold K-sparing drugs, recheck in 1 week |
| Orthostatic hypotension (SBP drop ≥20) | Reduce doses, check medications |
