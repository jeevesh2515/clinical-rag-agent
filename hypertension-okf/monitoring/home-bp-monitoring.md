# Home BP Monitoring (HBPM) Protocol

## Device Requirements
- **Upper-arm (brachial) cuff** — wrist and finger devices are not recommended
- **Validated device** — check at validatebp.org or stridebp.org
- **Appropriate cuff size** — bladder should encircle 80–100% of arm circumference
- Avoid devices that require smartphone app pairing unless validated

## Measurement Protocol

### Patient Instructions
1. **Empty bladder** before measurement
2. **Sit quietly** for ≥5 minutes, back supported, feet flat, legs uncrossed
3. **Cuff** at heart level, on bare arm (not over clothing)
4. **No talking** during measurement
5. **No caffeine, smoking, or exercise** within 30 minutes

### For Diagnosis (NICE Protocol)
- **Measure twice daily** (morning and evening) for **7 consecutive days**
- Morning: within 1 hour of waking, before medication, before breakfast
- Evening: before bedtime
- **Discard day 1 readings**
- Average the remaining readings to get diagnostic value

### For Monitoring Treated HTN
- **Measure twice daily** for **4–7 days** before a clinic visit
- OR measure for **24 hours** every 2–4 weeks
- Take two readings each time, 1–2 minutes apart; use the average

## Home BP Thresholds

| Category | Home BP |
|----------|---------|
| Normal | <130/85 |
| HTN diagnosis (NICE) | ≥135/85 (average over 7 days) |
| Target for treated HTN | <135/85 |
| Target (elderly ≥80) | <145/85 |

## Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Correction |
|---------|------------|
| Cuff too small | Use large adult cuff; underestimate BP by up to 10 mmHg with wrong size |
| Arm below heart level | Overestimates BP by up to 10 mmHg |
| Not resting 5 minutes | Overestimates by 5–10 mmHg |
| Discussing symptoms during measurement | Overestimates by 5–10 mmHg |
| Not discarding day 1 | Anxiety on first day inflates readings |
| Wrist monitor | Less accurate; use brachial cuff |
| Device not validated | May be inaccurate by ≥5 mmHg |
