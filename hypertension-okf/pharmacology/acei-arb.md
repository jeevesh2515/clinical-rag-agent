# ACE Inhibitors and ARBs

## Common ACE Inhibitors (with doses)

| Drug | Starting Dose | Target/Max Dose | Frequency |
|------|--------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Lisinopril | 10 mg | 20–40 mg | Daily |
| Enalapril | 5 mg | 10–40 mg | 1–2× daily |
| Ramipril | 2.5 mg | 10 mg | 1–2× daily |
| Perindopril | 4 mg | 8 mg | Daily |

## Common ARBs (with doses)

| Drug | Starting Dose | Target/Max Dose | Frequency |
|------|--------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Losartan | 50 mg | 100 mg | 1–2× daily |
| Candesartan | 8 mg | 32 mg | Daily |
| Valsartan | 80 mg | 160–320 mg | Daily |
| Telmisartan | 40 mg | 80 mg | Daily |
| Irbesartan | 150 mg | 300 mg | Daily |

## Key Monitoring Parameters

- **Serum creatinine + eGFR** — 1–2 weeks after starting or titrating
- **Serum potassium** — ACEi/ARB can cause hyperkalemia (especially with CKD or K-sparing diuretics)
- **Re-check at every dose change and at least annually**

## Common Side Effects

| ACEi | ARB |
|------|-----|
| Dry cough (5–20%) | Much lower cough rate |
| Angioedema (rare, 0.1–0.7%) | Angioedema (very rare) |
| Hyperkalemia | Hyperkalemia |
| Acute kidney injury (if dehydrated or bilateral RAS) | Same |
| Teratogenic (pregnancy category D) | Teratogenic (pregnancy category D) |

## Contraindications

- **Pregnancy** (both classes) — discontinue immediately
- **Bilateral renal artery stenosis** (risk of acute renal failure)
- **History of angioedema** (ACEi) — switch to ARB
- **Severe hyperkalemia** (K+ >5.5) — hold or reduce
