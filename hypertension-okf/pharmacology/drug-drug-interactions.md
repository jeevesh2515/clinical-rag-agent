# Key Drug-Drug Interactions

## High-Severity Interactions

| Drug/Class | Interacting With | Effect | Management |
|------------|-----------------|--------|------------|
| ACEi / ARB | **K-sparing diuretics** (spironolactone, amiloride, eplerenone) | ↑↑ Risk of hyperkalemia | Monitor K+ closely; avoid K+ supplements; use low dose spironolactone (≤25 mg) if K+ ≤4.5 |
| ACEi / ARB | **NSAIDs** (ibuprofen, naproxen, diclofenac, COX-2 inhibitors) | ↓ Antihypertensive effect; ↑ risk of AKI and hyperkalemia | Avoid NSAIDs if possible; use acetaminophen if needed; monitor renal function and BP |
| ACEi / ARB | **Potassium supplements** | ↑ Risk of hyperkalemia | Avoid routine potassium supplementation |
| NSAIDs | **All antihypertensives** | ↓ BP control (blunts effect); ↑ risk of fluid retention, HF exacerbation | Limit NSAID use; monitor BP and renal function |
| Verapamil / Diltiazem | **Beta-blockers** | ↑ Risk of bradycardia, heart block, hypotension | Avoid combination or monitor HR closely |
| Verapamil / Diltiazem | **Digoxin** | ↑ Digoxin levels (↑ toxicity risk) | Monitor digoxin levels; reduce digoxin dose |
| Verapamil / Diltiazem | **Cyclosporine / Tacrolimus** | ↑ Calcineurin inhibitor levels | Monitor levels; may need dose reduction |
| Thiazide diuretics | **Lithium** | ↑ Lithium levels (decreased renal clearance) | Monitor lithium levels; adjust dose |
| Thiazide diuretics | **Amiodarone** | ↑ Risk of QT prolongation (via hypokalemia) | Monitor K+, ECG if on both |

## Moderate-Severity Interactions

| Drug/Class | Interacting With | Effect | Management |
|------------|-----------------|--------|------------|
| ACEi / ARB | **Aliskiren** | ↑ Risk of hyperkalemia, hypotension, AKI | Avoid combination (contraindicated in diabetes) |
| CCB (amlodipine) | **Simvastatin** | ↑ Simvastatin levels (↑ myopathy risk) | Limit simvastatin to 20 mg/day or switch to atorvastatin/rosuvastatin |
| CCB (non-DHP) | **Simvastatin / Lovastatin** | ↑ Statin levels (↑ myopathy/rhabdomyolysis risk) | Avoid >20 mg simvastatin; switch to alternative statin |
| Beta-blockers | **Epinephrine** | Severe hypertension, bradycardia (non-cardioselective BB) | Use cardioselective BB if needed |
| Beta-blockers | **Insulin / sulfonylureas** | Masked hypoglycemia symptoms (tachycardia) | Educate patient; monitor glucose |
| Spironolactone | **Other K-sparing diuretics** | ↑↑ Hyperkalemia | Avoid combination |
| Spironolactone | **Trimethoprim** | ↑ Hyperkalemia risk (especially in elderly, CKD) | Monitor K+ if short course needed |

## Interactions with Common Non-CV Drugs

| Antihypertensive | Drug | Effect |
|-----------------|------|--------|
| All | **Oral contraceptives** | May ↑ BP; use alternative contraception in women with HTN |
| All | **Corticosteroids** | ↑ BP, fluid retention; monitor |
| All | **Decongestants** (pseudoephedrine, phenylephrine) | ↑ BP; avoid in uncontrolled HTN |
| All | **MAOIs** | ↑ Risk of hypertensive crisis with some agents; avoid |
| All | **Alcohol (>3 drinks/day)** | ↑ BP, reduced drug effectiveness |
