# First-Line Drug Classes and Indications

## Four Major Drug Classes

### ACE Inhibitors (ACEi)
- Examples: Lisinopril, Enalapril, Ramipril, Perindopril
- Mechanism: Blocks angiotensin II production
- Key trials: HOPE, EUROPA, PROGRESS
- Preferred in: CKD (any race), diabetes, heart failure, CAD, post-MI

### Angiotensin Receptor Blockers (ARB)
- Examples: Losartan, Candesartan, Valsartan, Telmisartan, Irbesartan
- Mechanism: Blocks angiotensin II receptor (AT1)
- Key trials: LIFE, RENAAL, IDNT, VALUE
- Preferred in: ACEi intolerance (cough, angioedema), CKD, heart failure

### Calcium Channel Blockers (CCB)
- Dihydropyridine: Amlodipine, Nifedipine LA, Felodipine
- Non-dihydropyridine: Diltiazem, Verapamil
- Mechanism: Vasodilation (DHP); rate control (non-DHP)
- Preferred in: Black patients (≥55), elderly, isolated systolic HTN, angina

### Thiazide / Thiazide-Like Diuretics
- Thiazide-like (preferred): Chlorthalidone, Indapamide
- Thiazide: Hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ)
- Mechanism: Inhibits Na+/Cl- reabsorption in distal tubule
- Preferred in: Black patients, elderly, isolated systolic HTN, heart failure

## Population-Specific First-Line Choice

| Population | First-Line | Notes |
|------------|------------|-------|
| Non-black, <55 years | ACEi or ARB | RAS blocker first |
| Non-black, ≥55 years | CCB or thiazide-like | Consider combination |
| Black (any age) | CCB or thiazide-like | RAS blockers less effective as monotherapy |
| CKD (albuminuria) | ACEi or ARB | Renoprotective |
| Diabetes | ACEi or ARB | Preferred if albuminuria |
| Heart failure | ACEi/ARB + beta-blocker + diuretic | Spironolactone if advanced |
| Pregnancy | Labetalol, Nifedipine, Methyldopa | Avoid ACEi/ARB |
| Isolated Systolic HTN | CCB or thiazide | Effective in elderly |
