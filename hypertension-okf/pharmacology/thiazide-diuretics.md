# Thiazide and Thiazide-Like Diuretics

## Drug Comparisons

| Drug | Class | Starting Dose | Max Dose | Frequency |
|------|-------|--------------|----------|-----------|
| **Chlorthalidone** | Thiazide-like (preferred) | 12.5 mg | 25–50 mg | Daily |
| **Indapamide** | Thiazide-like (preferred) | 1.25 mg | 2.5 mg | Daily |
| Hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ) | Thiazide | 12.5–25 mg | 50 mg | Daily |
| Bendroflumethiazide | Thiazide | 2.5 mg | 5 mg | Daily |

## Which Is Preferred?

- **Thiazide-like** (chlorthalidone, indapamide) are **preferred** over HCTZ
  - Longer half-life → better 24-hour BP control
  - Stronger evidence for cardiovascular outcome reduction (ALLHAT, ADVANCE)
  - HCTZ is weaker and may not provide 24-hour coverage

## Monitoring

| Parameter | Frequency | Action if abnormal |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Potassium, Sodium | 1–2 weeks after start/titrate, then annually | Hold if K+ <3.0 or Na+ <125 |
| Creatinine/eGFR | 1–2 weeks after start/titrate | May increase creatinine (reversible) |
| Uric acid | If gout suspected | Avoid or use cautiously in gout |
| Blood glucose | Annually | May worsen glucose tolerance |

## Common Side Effects

- Hypokalemia (dose-dependent, lower with thiazide-like)
- Hyponatremia (especially elderly)
- Hyperuricemia / gout flare
- Hypercalcemia (mild)
- Dyslipidemia (mild, transient)
- Erectile dysfunction (less than beta-blockers)

## Contraindications

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Gout (active) | Avoid; use alternative class |
| Severe hyponatremia (<125) | Avoid |
| Anuria / severe renal impairment (eGFR <30) | Generally ineffective; use loop diuretic |
| Symptomatic hypercalcemia | Avoid |
