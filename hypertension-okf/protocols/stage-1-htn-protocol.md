# Stage 1 HTN Protocol

## Definition
- **ACC/AHA**: BP 130–139 / 80–89 mmHg
- **NICE**: Clinic BP ≥140/90 confirmed by ABPM daytime avg ≥135/85 or HBPM avg ≥135/85
- **ESC/ESH**: Office BP 140–159 / 90–99 mmHg

## When to Start Drug Treatment

| Guideline | Criteria | Action |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| **ACC/AHA** | Stage 1 + ASCVD risk ≥10% OR clinical CVD/CKD/diabetes | Start drug therapy + lifestyle |
| **ACC/AHA** | Stage 1 + ASCVD risk <10% | Lifestyle only, recheck 3–6 months |
| **NICE** | Stage 1 + target organ damage, CVD, renal disease, diabetes, or 10-yr QRISK ≥10% | Offer drug treatment |
| **NICE** | Stage 1 + no risk factors | Lifestyle advice, recheck ABPM/HBPM annually |
| **ESC/ESH** | Grade 1 HTN + high/very high risk OR target organ damage | Start drug therapy |
| **ESC/ESH** | Grade 1 HTN + low-moderate risk | Lifestyle 3–6 months; drug if still elevated |

## Initial Drug Choice

| Population | First Agent |
|------------|-------------|
| Non-black, <55 years | ACEi or ARB |
| Non-black, ≥55 years | CCB or thiazide-like diuretic |
| Black (any age) | CCB or thiazide-like diuretic |
| CKD with albuminuria | ACEi or ARB (titrate to max tolerated) |

## Lifestyle Modifications

| Intervention | Target | Expected BP Reduction |
|-------------|--------|----------------------|
| Sodium restriction | <2g (≈5g salt) per day | 5–10 mmHg |
| Weight loss | BMI <25 | 5–20 mmHg per 10kg loss |
| DASH diet | Rich in fruits, vegetables, low-fat dairy | 8–14 mmHg |
| Physical activity | ≥150 min/week moderate aerobic | 5–8 mmHg |
| Alcohol | ≤2 drinks/day (men), ≤1 (women) | 4–8 mmHg |
| Smoking cessation | Stop completely | Reduced CVD risk |

## Follow-Up After Starting Treatment

- Recheck BP at **2–4 weeks** after starting or changing dose
- Check labs: creatinine, eGFR, K+, Na+ (2–4 weeks post-ACEi/ARB or diuretic start)
- If BP still above goal after **4–6 weeks** at max tolerated dose → proceed to [[protocols/step-care-treatment]]
