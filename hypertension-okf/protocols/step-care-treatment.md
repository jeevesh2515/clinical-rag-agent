# Step-Care Treatment Ladder

## NICE Stepped Approach (for adults <80)

| Step | Regimen | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Step 1** | ACEi or ARB (if <55, non-black) **OR** CCB (if ≥55 or black) | Monotherapy start |
| **Step 2** | ACEi/ARB + CCB | Dual therapy |
| **Step 3** | ACEi/ARB + CCB + **thiazide-like diuretic** (chlorthalidone or indapamide) | Triple therapy |
| **Step 4** | Add **spironolactone** (if K+ ≤4.5) **OR** alpha-blocker (doxazosin) **OR** beta-blocker | Resistant HTN |

## ACC/AHA Step-Care

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Step 1** | Lifestyle + single-pill combination (if BP ≥150/10 above goal) or monotherapy |
| **Step 2** | Titrate to max tolerated doses |
| **Step 3** | Add third agent from different class |
| **Step 4** | Add spironolactone or other mineralocorticoid antagonist; consider HTN specialist referral |

## ESC/ESH Step-Care

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Step 1** | Single-pill combination (ACEi/ARB + CCB or thiazide-like) **preferred** over monotherapy |
| **Step 2** | Triple combination: ACEi/ARB + CCB + thiazide-like |
| **Step 3** | Add spironolactone (25–50 mg) or another diuretic, or alpha-blocker, or beta-blocker |

## Step-Down (De-Escalation)

| When | How |
|------|-----|
| BP well-controlled for ≥12 months on ≥3 agents | Consider reducing one agent at a time |
| BP well-controlled on 2 agents | Consider reducing to monotherapy (especially if side effects) |
| BP well-controlled on 1 agent | Consider dose reduction (especially in elderly) |
| **Never stop ACEi/ARB abruptly** in HF or CKD | Taper if needed |

## Resistant Hypertension Definition

- BP not at goal despite **≥3 antihypertensives** from different classes (including a diuretic)
- Exclude: white coat effect, medication non-adherence, suboptimal regimen, secondary HTN
